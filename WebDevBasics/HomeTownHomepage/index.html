The European Central Bank

Frankfurt am Main is home to the European Central bank (ECB), which manages the euro and frames and implements EU economic & monetary policy.
It is one of several major banking institutions having settled in Frankfurt, making it one of Europe's premier financial center.

The status that Frankfurt enjoys today stems from its centuries-old role as a trading centre, which it largely acquired through its central geographical location and proximity to the Rhein river. Although finance and banking have supplanted commerce as the city main activity, it continuous to attract businesses worldwide.

Frankfurt currenlty counts 60.000 companies of different sizes, providing around 650,000 jobs. Consequently, its population of around 700.000 is notoriously cosmopolitan: as of 2015, a majority of the city residents have an at least part non-German background.
